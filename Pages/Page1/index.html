Assignment 1 : Color Compositions

This assignment was an extensive one. We were given gouache colors that represented a cool and a warm version of a primary and asked to blend them to get the primary and secondary colors. These colors were:

  • Rose Tyrien (Cool) + Winsor Red (Warm) = Red
  • Turquoise Blue (Cool) + Ultramarine (Warm) = Blue
  • Spectrum Yellow (Cool) + Brilliant Yellow (Warm) = Yellow
  • Rose Tyrien (Cool) + Ultramarine (Warm) = Violet
  • Turquoise Blue (Cool) + Spectrum Yellow (Cool) = Green
  • Winsor Red (Warm) + Brilliant Yellow (Warm) = Orange

and then the tertiary colors which are in between:

  • Green + Spectrum Yellow = Yellow Green
  • Green + Turquoise Blue = Blue Green
  • Orange + Winsor Red (Red Orange
  • Orange + Brilliant Yellow (Yellow Orange)
  • Violet + Rose Tyrien (Red Violet)
  • Violet + Ultramarine (Blue Violet)

After finishing these painted colors, we were then asked to match them digitally and print those out to see the closeness of the colors.


Afterwards, we were then asked to use this color wheel we built, make tinted versions and shaded versions and to make three-color schemes and four-color schemes based off triangles.





































The final part of the assignment involved taking these palettes and applying them to a composition. The composition I made was extremely geometric with a lack of overlay. This caused it to lack any real depth but would play well into what we were going to do with the colors...

























































We had four color schemes. One warm three-color, one cool three-color, one warm-four color scheme, and one cool-four cool. Then using tints and shades of the colors within this scheme, we were asked to change the relationship between our colors and push some into the background and some forward. Thanks to the nature of my composition, it allowed for a lot of experimentation in what to push forward and what to pull back. However, one issue of this was that because everything was near the same size, the lack of hierarchy made this push-pull game entirely dependent on color which sometimes was not enough to complete the task.

Looking back, If I could experiment more with it, I would play more with hierarchy and maybe have less forms in my work. Allowing for an easy time pushing and pulling through color.

Assignment 4 : Afterimage Effect

For this project, we were asked to explore "the afterimage effect". This is where a color touching another color can cause it to to turn a different hue, change in value, or intensity. We had to start by choosing a form (i.e zodiac sign) and then divide it into two equal sections. Afterwards, apply a warm color to one side and a cool color to the other side, making sure that both colors are of equal value despite being different temperatures. Then after choosing these colors, put the divided form within four sets of 3"x"3 equally divided. Then we had to choose colors for the background based off of these requirements:

  • Two light colors of equal value
  • One dark color on one side and one color of the same value as the sign on the other side
  • One warm bright color on one side and one dull, cool color on the other side
  • Final set has to use the compliments for each section and then afterwards the entire 8"x8" is placed on a grey square that is the same value as the final sign.



For my symbol I chose none other than the infamous Aki! The very person teaching this class. As for the colors chosen of the same value, I chose a purple and an orange since I felt those were the colors that could best be used for the afterimage effect. After finishing and making sure the values are right, then we had to separate the process colors to show how much of each goes into every part of the image.







































Finally we had to pick out all the colors we used and match them with their pantone as well as half-tone the image.






I found this project to be the most technical out of all the other projects however through it I learned a lot of settings I had not known on illustrator and it helped me gain a stronger understanding of my printer as well as some of the technicalities within the relationships between colors.
